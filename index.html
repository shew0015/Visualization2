Exploring Windspeed in the 8 Major Cities of Australia

Windspeed (km/hr)

This section explores the windspeed (km/hr) averaged over 10 minutes before 9 am in the 8 major cities of Australia.

Wind Speed (km/hr) Averaged Over 10 Minutes Prior to 9:00 a.m. in the 8 Major Cities of Australia From 2008 to 2016

Analysis:

Based on the results above, Melbourne had the highest yearly averaged windspeed (km/hr) prior to 9:00 a.m. from 2008 to 2016, Brisbane had the lowest yearly averaged windspeed (km/hr) prior to 9:00 a.m. from 2008 to 2016.

Windspeed (km/hr) is measured by the difference in air pressure between two points. This indicates that the air pressure differences in Melbourne is high. The highest yearly averaged windspeed (km/hr) prior to 9:00 a.m. from 2008 to 2016 in Melbourne is 21.16 km/hr, and the year is 2012.

Windspeed (km/hr) is affected by factors such as temperature, centripetal force, rotation of Earth, etc. Windspeed might also be affected by other weather conditions such as humidity (%), pressure (hPa). The next section explores the correlation between humidity (%) and windspeed (km/hr), and the correlation between pressure (hPa) and windspeed (km/hr).
